Cooling

Additional sections below.
Cooling is a small, private mobile app for two people. Either person can perform a single action called Chomp. A Chomp sends a quiet buzz to the other person. The notification contains no message, explanation, or instruction. A Chomp also places something in the oven for the sender for exactly 108 seconds. While something is in the oven, the app shows "in the oven • N seconds". When the oven time ends, the app returns to "Cooling". There is no chat. There are no replies. There is no obligation to respond.
Cooling never names what is in the oven. It may be affection, irritation, longing, attention, grief, or nothing in particular. Naming it would turn it into content. Measuring it would turn it into work. The app leaves it unspecified on purpose. Each person's oven is independent. There is no turn-taking and no synchronization. It shows only one historical signal: "last chomp: just now / 12m ago / 3h ago / yesterday". The app enforces restraint quietly and without reward. The oven time is finite and precise. Everything else is cooling.
Cooling deliberately avoids messaging threads, read receipts, presence indicators, analytics, streaks, reminders, and growth mechanics. These features convert care into labor and attention into performance. Cooling refuses that conversion.
Cooling is complete by design. It has no roadmap. It does not seek improvement through addition. There is no recipe. If it adds replies, metrics, optimization, or explanation, it should be considered broken. Cooling is everything besides putting something briefly in the oven.
